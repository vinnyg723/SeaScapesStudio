Taxonomy: Perciformes, Clownfish, Damselfish (Pomacentridae)

General Information: The damsels and clowns include about 320 species in 28 genera. They are nearly entirely marine with only a few brackish species. They have a slender oval body, and serrated scales, often with brilliant colors.

Diet: Most species are omnivores, feeding on plankton and algae.

Final Note:It is important to know the species before introducing it to a home aquarium. Some damsels and most clowns are very territorial. Clowns will do best if their symbiont anemone is present. Some species - Stegastes and Eupomacentrus will nip at the small polyps of stony corals.
